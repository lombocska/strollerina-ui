## When to Buy a Stroller

Buying a stroller ahead of your child’s arrival ensures you’re prepared for any outing, from a simple stroll to longer journeys. It’s worth researching and testing models early so you have ample time to make the best decision for your needs.

---

## How to Choose the Right Stroller

### Key Considerations for Stroller Selection

- **Purpose and Lifestyle:** Assess how and where you’ll use the stroller. For urban settings, compact and maneuverable models are ideal. For outdoor activities, all-terrain strollers with durable wheels are preferable.
  
- **Safety Features:** Look for a sturdy frame, a 5-point harness, and a reliable braking system. Ensure the stroller meets international safety standards.
  
- **Foldability and Storage:** A stroller that folds easily and compactly can be a lifesaver, especially for parents on the go or with limited storage space.
  
- **Age and Weight Compatibility:** Consider strollers that grow with your child, such as those with convertible designs or adjustable seating positions.
  
- **Comfort for Baby and Parent:** Padded seats, adjustable reclines, and ample storage for parent essentials are valuable features.
  
- **Travel System Compatibility:** Many strollers are compatible with car seats, creating a seamless travel system for infants.

## Stroller Categories

### Full-Size Strollers

Designed for everyday use, these strollers offer ample storage, adjustable features, and often compatibility with car seats for newborns.

### Lightweight Strollers

Ideal for travel or quick trips, lightweight strollers are easy to carry, fold, and store. They’re best suited for toddlers or older babies.

### Jogging Strollers

Built for active parents, jogging strollers have large, air-filled tires and shock-absorbing suspension systems for running or navigating bumpy terrain.

### Double Strollers

Perfect for twins or siblings close in age, these strollers come in tandem or side-by-side configurations.

---

## Practical Tips

- **Test Before You Buy:** If possible, test strollers in-store to ensure ease of handling and folding.
- **Check the Wheels:** Larger wheels offer better maneuverability on rough surfaces, while swivel wheels provide easier navigation in tight spaces.
- **Adjustable Handles:** If multiple caregivers will use the stroller, adjustable handles accommodate different heights.
